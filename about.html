Mapping The Visible

Gravitional lensing effect is a direct consequence of Einstein's theory of relavity.

Strong Lensing, in particular, occurs when a large celestial body lies directly between observer and a lens source.

Strong Lensing reveals the nature of dark matter-non-barytonic matter that does not interact with electromagnetic effect.

This research uses Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithm to construct lens models, thereby measuring dark matter content

A complex target, Abell 1689 is experimented with this method, archiving high precision in measureing DM content successfully.

This website also features strong gravitational lensing blog which includes possible lensing canadiates for research.
